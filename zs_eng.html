Charming Zhongshan City：
Birthplace of Sun Yat-sen & Pearl of the Bay Area

Zhongshan City, Guangdong Province, formerly known as Xiangshan. This land of outstanding people and natural beauty has produced many famous figures and is the hometown of the great Sun Yat-sen. Zhongshan is located in south-central Guangdong Province. It lies at the mouth of the lower reaches of the Xijiang and Beijiang in the south-central part of the Pearl River Delta. Geographically positioned between latitudes 22°11′ - 22°47′ N and longitudes 113°09′ - 113°46′ E, the city is highly accessible and well-connected. Zhongshan shares its borders with Guangzhou and Foshan to the north, and Jiangmen and Zhuhai to the west and south. To the east, it looks out across the Lingdingyang waters of the Pearl River Estuary, directly facing Shenzhen and Hong Kong.

History of Zhongshan

Before 1925, Zhongshan was known as Xiangshan. According to the book Taiping Huanyu Ji from the Song Dynasty, it is recorded that Xiangshan was located 300 li south across the sea, blessed with an abundance of divine flowers. That’s why the area earned the name Xiangshan, meaning “Fragrant Mountain.”

In ancient times, Xiangshan was an isolated island floating in the Lingdingyang, just outside the Pearl River Estuary. Its territory was limited to the hills and mountains around today's Wugui Mountain and Fenghuang Mountain, stretching from Shiqi district down to Macau. Archaeological findings reveal that during the Neolithic period, over 5,000 years ago, the indigenous Gu Yue people lived a semi-settled life on Xiangshan Island, thriving on fishing and hunting.

Throughout early history, the region's administration shifted: it fell under Panyu County during the Han Dynasty, Dongguan Commandery after the Jin Dynasty, and Dongguan County during the Tang Dynasty. In 1152, during the Southern Song Dynasty, the government officially established Xiangshan County. This new county incorporated coastal areas from Nanhai, Panyu, and Xinhui, and was governed by Guangzhou. During the Republic of China era, Xiangshan County came under the direct administration of Guangdong Province. It was here, in the village of Cuiheng, that the great revolutionary leader Sun Yat-sen was born. Following Sun Yat-sen’s passing on March 12, 1925, the Generalissimo Headquarters of the Army and Navy of the Republic of China in Guangzhou made a historic decision. On April 15 of that year, Xiangshan County was officially renamed Zhongshan County to honor his incredible legacy. After the founding of The People's Republic of China in October 1949, the area retained the name Zhongshan County and was administered by the Foshan region. With the approval of the State Council, it was reorganized into a county-level city in December 1983. In January 1988, following further approval by the State Council, Zhongshan was elevated to a prefecture-level city under the direct jurisdiction of Guangdong Province.
